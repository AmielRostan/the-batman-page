The Batman

Origin:

The character of Batman originated in 1939 when he was created by Bob Kane and Bill Finger. He was conceived as a response to the success of Superman, the first superhero, with a darker and more detective-oriented approach. Inspired by characters like Zorro and The Shadow, Batman is the alter ego of Bruce Wayne, a billionaire who vowed to avenge his parents' death and fight crime in Gotham City. His creation marked the beginning of the Golden Age of comics and laid the foundation for the emergence of an iconic character that has endured over the years in comics, movies, TV series, and other media.

Real identity:

Batman's true identity is Bruce Wayne, a wealthy philanthropist and the heir to the Wayne family fortune. He leads a double life, using his vast resources to fund his crime-fighting activities as Batman. Wayne's motivation to become the Caped Crusader stems from witnessing the murder of his parents as a child, which instilled in him a deep desire for justice and a commitment to protecting Gotham City from crime and corruption. His dual identity allows him to navigate the criminal underworld while maintaining the façade of a billionaire playboy during the day.
